## Introduction

This handbook outlines the policies, expectations, and procedures that govern employment at Contoso. All employees are required to read, understand, and comply with the policies contained herein. Questions should be directed to your HR Business Partner or the People & Culture team at hr@contoso.com.

---

## Policy HR-101: Code of Conduct

All Contoso employees are expected to conduct themselves professionally and ethically at all times. This includes interactions with colleagues, clients, partners, and vendors.

**Core expectations:**
- Treat all individuals with respect and dignity regardless of role, background, or tenure
- Protect confidential company and client information
- Avoid conflicts of interest; disclose potential conflicts to your manager and HR immediately
- Do not accept gifts valued at more than $50 from vendors or clients without prior written approval from your manager and Finance
- Represent Contoso honestly and accurately in all communications

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

---

## Policy HR-102: Workplace Harassment and Non-Discrimination

Contoso prohibits harassment, discrimination, and retaliation in any form. Protected characteristics include race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, sexual orientation, gender identity, and veteran status.

**Reporting:** Any employee who experiences or witnesses harassment or discrimination should report it immediately to their HR Business Partner, their manager (if not the subject of the complaint), or through the anonymous Ethics Hotline at 1-800-555-0190 or ethics.contoso.com.

All reports are investigated promptly and confidentially. Retaliation against anyone who reports in good faith is itself a policy violation and grounds for discipline.

---

## Policy HR-103: Reporting Structure and Organizational Hierarchy

Contoso operates under a matrix reporting structure. Every employee has a direct-line manager responsible for day-to-day work and performance, and may have a dotted-line functional leader for cross-functional initiatives.

- Org charts are maintained in Workday and updated within five business days of any change
- Employees may escalate concerns to their skip-level manager if they are unable to resolve an issue with their direct manager
- Any changes to reporting relationships must be approved by the relevant VP and submitted through an HR Business Partner

---

## Policy HR-104: Performance Review Process

Contoso conducts formal performance reviews on a semi-annual cycle: mid-year reviews in July and annual reviews in January.

**Mid-Year Review (July):**
- Manager and employee complete self-assessments in Workday
- One-on-one calibration meeting between employee and manager
- No compensation changes result from mid-year reviews
- Outcomes: goal adjustments, development plans

**Annual Review (January):**
- Includes rating on a five-point scale: Exceptional, Exceeds Expectations, Meets Expectations, Needs Improvement, Unsatisfactory
- Compensation adjustments and bonus eligibility tied to annual rating
- Rating of "Meets Expectations" or higher required to be eligible for merit increase
- Ratings are calibrated across peer groups by department leadership

**Continuous feedback** is encouraged through Workday's Check-In tool. Managers are expected to conduct one-on-ones at least bi-weekly.

## Policy HR-105: Disciplinary Procedures

Contoso uses a progressive discipline model for most policy violations. The stages are:

1. **Verbal Warning** — Documented in Workday by the manager. Employee is notified verbally and a written record is kept.
2. **Written Warning** — Formal written notice outlining the violation, expectations, and improvement timeline. Employee signature required as acknowledgment (not agreement).
3. **Final Written Warning / Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** — A 30- to 90-day structured improvement plan with specific, measurable targets. Failure to meet PIP targets may result in termination.
4. **Termination** — Separation from employment.

Contoso reserves the right to bypass any step in cases of gross misconduct, including but not limited to: theft, fraud, workplace violence, violation of HR-102, unauthorized disclosure of confidential information, or illegal activity.

All disciplinary actions must be reviewed with an HR Business Partner before being delivered to the employee.

## Policy HR-106: Remote and Hybrid Work

Contoso supports hybrid work arrangements where roles permit. The default expectation is a minimum of three in-office days per week for hybrid roles. Fully remote roles are designated at the time of hire and noted in the offer letter.

- Remote work arrangements outside of the approved hybrid schedule require manager and HR approval
- Employees are responsible for maintaining a safe and productive remote work environment
- Contoso-issued equipment must be used for all work activities; personal devices may only be used with IT approval and enrollment in MDM

---

## Policy HR-107: Confidentiality and Information Security

All employees must protect Contoso's confidential information, including trade secrets, client data, financial information, and personnel records. This obligation continues after separation from Contoso.

- Employees must complete annual Security Awareness Training in the Learning Management System (LMS) by March 31 each year
- Sharing confidential information via personal email or unauthorized file-sharing services is prohibited
- Any suspected data breach or security incident must be reported to security@contoso.com immediately

---

## Policy HR-108: Workplace Safety

Contoso is committed to maintaining a safe working environment. All employees must:
- Report unsafe conditions to Facilities at facilities@contoso.com or ext. 5500
- Participate in emergency drills when conducted
- Never threaten or engage in violence of any kind in the workplace
