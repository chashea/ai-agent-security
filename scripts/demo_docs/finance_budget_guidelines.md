# Contoso Annual Budget Planning Guidelines

**Last Updated: 2026-01-15**
**Classification: Internal**
**Owner: Financial Planning & Analysis**

---

## Overview

This document provides guidance for department leaders and finance business partners on Contoso's annual budget planning process. Adherence to the timeline and submission standards ensures accurate consolidated forecasts and board-ready materials.

---

## Fiscal Year Calendar

Contoso operates on a July–June fiscal year.

| Milestone | Target Date |
|---|---|
| FY planning kickoff | March 1 |
| Headcount plan submissions due | March 31 |
| OpEx submissions due | April 15 |
| CapEx submissions due | April 15 |
| FP&A consolidation and review | April 16 – May 15 |
| Executive review and challenge sessions | May 16 – May 31 |
| Board approval | June 15 |
| Budget locked | June 30 |

Any submission received after the deadline will not be included in the first consolidation pass and may result in reduced budget allocation.

---

## Department Submission Process

1. Finance Business Partners distribute budget templates to department heads by March 1.
2. Departments complete the headcount plan tab first — all approved open roles must be included.
3. OpEx and CapEx tabs are completed based on strategic priorities for the upcoming fiscal year.
4. Submissions are routed through the department head to the VP for sign-off before submission to FP&A.
5. All submissions must be made via the Budget Portal at `budget.contoso.internal`.

---

## CapEx vs. OpEx Classification

Misclassifying capital expenditures as operating expenses (or vice versa) affects financial statements and tax treatment. Use the following guidelines:

### Capital Expenditure (CapEx)
- Tangible assets with a useful life greater than 12 months and cost exceeding $5,000
- Software licenses purchased outright (perpetual)
- Hardware: servers, networking equipment, workstations
- Leasehold improvements

### Operating Expenditure (OpEx)
- SaaS subscriptions (monthly or annual)
- Professional services and consulting fees
- Travel and entertainment
- Marketing campaigns and programs
- Maintenance contracts and support agreements
- Any individual item under $5,000

When classification is unclear, contact your Finance Business Partner before submitting.

---

## Headcount Planning

- All headcount additions must be modeled by start quarter, not start month.
- Include fully loaded cost (base salary + benefits at 28% + equity amortization).
- Backfills for known attrition should be included with a note referencing the departing role.
- Contractor and contingent worker costs are classified as OpEx, not headcount.
- FTE headcount freeze periods (if any) will be communicated separately by HR.

---

## Variance Reporting

During the fiscal year, departments are expected to track and report budget-to-actual variances monthly.

| Variance Threshold | Required Action |
|---|---|
| Under 5% favorable or unfavorable | No action required |
| 5% – 10% | Written explanation in monthly flash report |
| 10% – 20% | Finance Business Partner review and reforecast |
| Above 20% | VP sign-off on reforecast; CFO may request presentation |

Variances driven by timing (accelerated or delayed spend) must be clearly flagged as timing vs. run-rate in all reporting.

---

## Budget Reallocation Requests

In-year reallocation requests (moving budget between cost centers or GL categories) require:

- Written justification submitted via the Budget Portal
- Manager approval for reallocations under $25,000
- VP approval for reallocations $25,000 – $250,000
- CFO approval for reallocations above $250,000

Reallocations between CapEx and OpEx budgets require Finance Director approval regardless of amount, due to accounting treatment implications.
