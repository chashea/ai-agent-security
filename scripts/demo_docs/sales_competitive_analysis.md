## Overview

## Acme Corp

**Positioning:** "The trusted platform for enterprise automation" — Acme leads with brand name and a large system integrator (SI) ecosystem. Frequently appears on Gartner Magic Quadrant.

**Pricing:** Starts at $40/user/month for standard tier. Enterprise pricing is module-based and notoriously complex, leading to bill shock post-implementation.

**Strengths:**
- Recognized brand that clears procurement review quickly
- 400+ SI and technology partners
- Broad feature surface covering many use cases

**Weaknesses:**
- Product built on a legacy architecture (acquired 2017); technical debt is visible in UI and performance
- Roadmap moves slowly — 2 major releases per year
- Customer support rated poorly in G2 reviews (3.1/5)
- High total cost of ownership when professional services are included

**Win/Loss vs. Acme:** Win rate 58%. We tend to win on product modernity, time-to-value, and support quality. We lose when the prospect has an existing Acme relationship or when procurement requires a recognized brand.

**Competitive Talk Track:**
- Ask about their current Acme implementation and customization footprint — heavy customization increases switching cost perception, but also surfaces dissatisfaction.
- Highlight our API-first architecture vs. Acme's proprietary integration model.
- Offer a side-by-side demo focused on configuration speed.

## GlobalTech

**Positioning:** "Enterprise-grade at every level" — GlobalTech sells to Fortune 500 IT and Procurement buyers. Average deal cycle is 12–18 months with mandatory professional services.

**Pricing:** Opaque enterprise pricing. Average ACV in our shared deal data is $450K. Always bundled with $150K+ implementation services.

**Strengths:**
- Deep ERP integrations (SAP, Oracle, Workday)
- Strong compliance certifications (FedRAMP, ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, HIPAA)
- Large dedicated implementation team; perceived as lower risk for large transformations

**Weaknesses:**
- Mandatory professional services add 40–60% to total cost
- 12–18 month implementation timelines are common
- Poor self-service capabilities — customers feel dependent post-implementation
- SaaS product is still maturing; core product originated as on-premises

**Win/Loss vs. GlobalTech:** Win rate 41%. We win when the champion values speed-to-value and lower TCO. We lose when the buying committee is IT-led and risk-averse, or when GlobalTech's ERP integrations are a hard requirement.

**Competitive Talk Track:**
- Lead with total cost of ownership including implementation and ongoing services.
- Quantify time-to-value: Contoso average go-live in 6–8 weeks vs. GlobalTech 12–18 months.
- If ERP integration is a concern, confirm which specific integrations are needed — we cover the majority natively.

## NovaSoft

**Positioning:** "Simple, fast, affordable" — NovaSoft targets SMB and lower mid-market buyers who prioritize speed and price over depth. Not a frequent competitor in enterprise deals.

**Pricing:** Starter at $15/user/month. Max volume pricing around $35/user/month. No enterprise tier.

**Strengths:**
- Extremely fast onboarding (same-day trials, guided setup)
- Lowest price point in the market
- Simple UX with minimal training required

**Weaknesses:**
- No support for complex role hierarchies or multi-entity org structures
- Compliance features are basic — no SOC 2 audit trail, no data residency controls
- API rate limits and storage caps create scaling problems above 500 users
- No SLA guarantees below enterprise tier

**Win/Loss vs. NovaSoft:** Win rate 74% in enterprise. We rarely compete directly — when we see NovaSoft, it usually means the prospect is under-qualified or budget-constrained. Redirect conversation to long-term cost and scalability risk.

## PrimeSolutions

**Positioning:** "Built for regulated industries" — PrimeSolutions focuses on financial services, healthcare, and government with deep compliance and audit functionality.

**Pricing:** $80–$150/user/month depending on compliance modules. Minimum contract $150K ACV.

**Strengths:**
- Best-in-class audit trail and compliance reporting
- Pre-built templates for FINRA, HIPAA, SOX, GDPR
- Strong reference base in financial services (used by 6 of top 10 US banks)

**Weaknesses:**
- Narrow product scope — primarily compliance workflow, not general-purpose automation
- UI is functional but dated; user adoption can be challenging outside compliance teams
- Limited extensibility — customization requires professional services
- Poor performance in non-regulated industry deals due to overfitting to compliance use cases

**Win/Loss vs. PrimeSolutions:** Win rate 65% outside regulated industries. In regulated industries, win rate drops to 38% — PrimeSolutions' reference base and compliance depth are hard to overcome. Focus competitive efforts on breadth of use case and total platform value.

**Competitive Talk Track:**
- Position Contoso as the platform that handles compliance AND broader operational workflows — less tool sprawl.
- For regulated industry deals, lead with our compliance certifications and reference the customers we have in that vertical.
- Ask about their automation needs beyond compliance — PrimeSolutions has no answer for general workflow.
