## Overview

This playbook provides Account Executives and Sales Development Representatives with a structured approach to prospecting, qualifying, and advancing deals through the Contoso sales motion. It covers the ideal customer profile, qualification framework, discovery call structure, objection handling, and demo best practices.

---

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Contoso's highest-converting, highest-retention customers share the following characteristics:

### Firmographics
- Company size: 250 – 5,000 employees (sweet spot: 500 – 2,000)
- Industry: Technology, Financial Services, Healthcare, Professional Services, Manufacturing
- Geography: North America (primary), Western Europe (secondary)
- Revenue: $50M – $2B annually

### Technographics
- Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace as primary productivity suite
- Using Salesforce, SAP, or Workday (indicates process maturity)
- Has an IT Security function (signals compliance awareness)
- No current automation platform, or using a legacy/point-solution tool

### Behavioral Signals
- Recent compliance initiative, audit finding, or regulatory change
- M&A activity (process consolidation need)
- Rapid headcount growth (>20% YoY)
- Job postings for workflow, automation, or operations roles
- Engaged with Contoso content (webinar, whitepaper, G2 review page visits)

## Qualification Framework (BANT)

Use BANT to qualify each opportunity before investing significant selling time.

### Budget
- Does the prospect have a budget allocated or identifiable? (Not necessarily approved yet.)
- Key question: "Is there an existing budget for tooling in this area, or would this be a new budget request?"
- Threshold: Opportunities below $30K ACV expected spend should be routed to inside sales or self-serve.

### Authority
- Are you speaking with a decision-maker or at least a strong champion with access to one?
- Key question: "Who else will be involved in evaluating and approving a solution like this?"
- Red flag: If the only contact is a junior IC with no connection to budget owner.

### Need
- Is there a clear, articulated problem that Contoso solves?
- Key question: "What's the cost of not solving this problem — in time, risk, or dollars?"
- Qualification threshold: Prospect can articulate a specific pain point, not just general curiosity.

### Timeline
- Is there a realistic decision timeline?
- Key question: "Is there a date by which you'd like to have a solution in place?"
- Green flag: Defined project or compliance deadline driving urgency.
- Yellow flag: "We're just exploring" with no stated timeline — keep in nurture.

## Discovery Call Template

### Pre-Call Preparation (15 minutes)
- Review the company's LinkedIn, website, and any recent news.
- Check Salesforce for any prior Contoso interactions or contacts.
- Review any forms submitted or content downloaded.
- Identify 2–3 hypothesis pain points based on ICP signals.

### Call Structure (45 minutes)

**Opening (5 min)**
- Agenda setting: "I'd like to spend the first 20 minutes learning about your current environment and challenges, and then share how we've helped similar companies. Sound good?"
- Build rapport briefly — shared connection, recent company news.

**Discovery (20 min)**
- "Walk me through how your team currently handles [relevant process] today."
- "What are the biggest pain points or friction points in that process?"
- "How is that affecting the business — what does it cost you in time, risk, or money?"
- "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"
- "Who else is impacted by this problem outside your team?"

**Qualification (10 min)**
- "Where does solving this rank among your priorities this quarter / this year?"
- "Is there a budget in place, or would this be a new investment?"
- "Who would be involved in evaluating and making this decision?"
- "What would success look like 6 months after implementing a solution?"

**Positioning (10 min)**
- Share 1–2 relevant customer stories (same industry or similar use case).
- Position Contoso's unique differentiators tied directly to stated pain.
- Do not run a product demo on the discovery call — earn the demo.

**Close / Next Steps (5 min)**
- "Based on what you've shared, I think we have a strong fit — here's what I'd suggest as a next step."
- Propose a specific next step with a date: technical demo, stakeholder call, or proof of concept scoping.

## Top 5 Objections and Responses

### 1. "We already have a solution for this."

**Response:** "That's helpful context — can you tell me more about what you're using today? Many of our customers came to us from [competitor or category]. What I often hear is that [common pain point with incumbent]. Is that something you're experiencing, or is the current solution working well for you?"

Goal: Open a gap. Don't dismiss their current solution — qualify whether it's actually solving the problem.

---

### 2. "Your price is too high."

**Response:** "I appreciate you being direct. Before I respond on price, can I ask — are you comparing us to a specific alternative, or is it more of an absolute budget constraint? I ask because our pricing is structured to reflect total value, and I want to make sure we're comparing apples to apples. Can we walk through the expected ROI together?"

Goal: Anchor on value, not price. Use the pricing guide to identify volume or term discounts. Involve your manager if needed.

---

### 3. "We don't have budget right now."

**Response:** "That's fair. A lot of our best customers started that conversation in a similar position. Can I ask — is this more of a timing issue, where budget would be available next quarter or next fiscal year? Or is it truly off the table? I want to make sure I understand whether this is a now conversation or a planning conversation."

Goal: Distinguish between no budget now (timing) vs. no budget period (disqualify or long-nurture).

---

### 4. "We need to see how it handles [specific requirement] before we commit."

**Response:** "Absolutely — that's a reasonable ask. Let's make sure we don't waste each other's time. Can you tell me more about that requirement specifically? If it's a fit, I'd like to propose a scoped proof of concept so you can validate it in your environment before making any decision."

Goal: Convert into a structured POC. Get a clear success criteria document before the POC starts.

---

### 5. "We're not ready to move forward right now — can you follow up in a few months?"

**Response:** "Of course — I want to respect your timeline. To make sure my follow-up is actually useful, can I ask what needs to change between now and then? If I know what's holding things back, I can time my outreach better and make sure I come back with something relevant."

Goal: Understand the real blocker. Set a specific follow-up date with a defined trigger event.

## Demo Best Practices

- Always tailor the demo to the prospect's stated pain points from discovery — never run a generic product tour.
- Begin with a "here's what we'll cover and why" frame based on their specific situation.
- Limit the demo to 3–4 core use cases. Depth beats breadth.
- Have a second SE or technical resource on the call for complex enterprise demos.
- Pause and ask for reactions every 10–12 minutes: "Does this reflect how your team would use it?"
- End every demo with: "What did you see today that was most relevant to your situation?" and "What questions do you still have?"
- Send a follow-up summary within 24 hours including key takeaways, relevant customer references, and proposed next steps.
